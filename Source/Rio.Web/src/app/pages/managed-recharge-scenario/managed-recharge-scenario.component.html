1. Managed Recharge Scenario

Managed Recharge Scenario

By integrating the Water Trading Platform with the Groundwater Evaluation Toolkit (GET), groundwater managers can better understand potential benefits and impacts of their groundwater recharge activities. For example, recent studies describe how groundwater subsidence due to over-pumping can release arsenic into productive aquifers where it can reach pumping wells. This can be imagined similar to the release of dirty water when a sponge is squeezed.

The map below shows a hypothetical scenario which increases groundwater level due to managed aquifer recharge at constructed recharge basins. The impacts of groundwater level changes can be evaluated considering to a variety of factors, including locations of known groundwater quality contamination, nearby disadvantaged communities (DACs), and location of private domestic wells. In this scenario:

  • Managed aquifer recharge is occurring at two Recharge Basins and 500 ac-ft is recharged uniformly throughout the year
  • Groundwater quality monitoring locations show areas where arsenic concentration exceed the regulatory limit of 10 ug/L
  • Changes to groundwater levels can also results in impacts to disadvantaged communities who rely on private domestic wells for their drinking water
