1. Measuring Water Use with OpenET

Measuring Water Use with OpenET

Tracking water use and demand

Water use calculations on this trading platform are provided by OpenET - a web-based platform being developed to provide easily accessible, credible estimates of consumptive water use at the field scale. OpenET relies on publicly available satellite and weather station data to estimate evapotranspiration (ET) - water that is transferred from the land to the atmosphere via evaporation from bare soil and transpiration through plant leaves. For irrigated agricultural fields, ET is necessary for plant growth and food production.

While several methods already exist for measuring ET at the field scale, they’re expensive, and since they’re calculated differently by every consulting group, there isn’t a lot of broad trust in the data. Rosedale-Rio Bravo chose to partner with OpenET because it is bringing leading scientists and some of the most commonly used approaches for estimating ET together in one place, helping to create a shared basis for decision-making.

When it launches publicly in 2021, OpenET will significantly increase access to ET data for individual farmers and landowners, giving them the opportunity to see the same data that water managers and regulators are using by providing low-cost, near real-time ET estimates through a widely accessible website. The OpenET platform is currently in a beta version, and data are being provided here for user testing purposes only. However, with further work on model intercomparison and refinements, the objective of OpenET is to provide broadly trusted ET estimates that are well-documented, easily retrieved, and truly representative of the best available science.

Rosedale Rio Bravo utilizes data from OpenET within this trading platform to account for consumptive use on each assessor’s parcel. This information allows the district to accurately account for and report the water consumptively used by each parcel, and gives landowners the ability to accurately track their water budgets throughout the year.

The OpenET Team

The OpenET project is being led by NASA, Desert Research Institute (DRI) and Environmental Defense Fund with in-kind support from Google Earth Engine. The technical team includes approximately 30 researchers and practitioners from NASA, DRI, the U.S. Department of Agriculture, the U.S. Geological Survey, University of Nebraska, University of Idaho, University of Wisconsin, University of Maryland, California State University Monterey Bay, Google Earth Engine and the web development firm HabitatSeven, with expertise in satellite-based estimation of evapotranspiration, cloud computing, and user-driven website design.

Multiple ET Models

OpenET is providing evapotranspiration data derived from several models that integrate satellite and meteorological data to generate estimates at the field scale. Because they are estimates, and because the models utilize different methodologies, they can produce results that diverge from field measurement. However, recent advances in remote sensing of ET have led to significant improvements in our ability to automate these methodologies, and many of these methods are already informing water use decisions across the West.

Creating an "ensemble" value

The OpenET project is integrating many of these advances to support further improvements in water management by increasing access to remotely sensed ET data, and building confidence in the data by developing an ensemble value that is endorsed by a community of scientists. The models selected for inclusion in the initial ensemble are already informing water use decisions across the West, but the OpenET project will make it much easier to compare the models at scale, allowing identification of consistent differences between the various methods and for the scientific community to further refine and improve the estimates.

The OpenET team is initiating one of the largest model intercomparison and accuracy assessments conducted to date to improve ET estimates. The results will inform the calculation of the single ensemble value from the full suite of ET models.

The data in the accounting platform represents the OpenET team’s preliminary estimate of an ensemble value based on the best available science. However, the science will significantly advance over the coming year as the intercomparison effort progresses and additional lessons are learned.
